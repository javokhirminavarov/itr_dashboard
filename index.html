It's everywhere

E-commerce is not a niche channel. Split 2025's caseload into the six ITR sections and every one of them carries an online tail — from IPR, Health & Safety at 67.8% down to AML/CTF at 1.0%. Even the floor isn't zero.

This is a structural property of the trade, not a specialist corner of it. Keep scrolling: each section bar splits into the commodities beneath it.

But wildly uneven

The 44.8% headline is an average, and the average conceals more than it reveals. Ranked flow by flow, e-commerce penetration runs from 81.9% for new psychoactive substances to effectively zero for hazardous waste. A spectrum, not a number.

Seven commodities clear 55% — everything from NPS down to khat. Below khat the share falls off a cliff, from 55% to 34% in a single step. That cliff is the real dividing line.

The ceiling — if it's mailable, it ships like retail

Hold the top cluster and dim the rest. The goods that live online share a profile: small, high-value, parcel-ready. Drill into their sub-commodities and the shares climb higher still.

Erectile-dysfunction medicines reach 91%, gun silencers 85%, e-cigarettes 64%. If a product fits in an envelope and carries retail margins, it moves like retail.

The floor — physical value stays physical

Invert the view. At the bottom sit alcohol (0.5%), gold (0.4%), waste (0.2%), cash (1.0%), ODS/HFC gases (0.6%) and drones (0.8%). Bulk weight, hand-to-hand value and dangerous cargo still move the old way.

Freight, couriers, concealment in the load. The line running down the spectrum is physical: what a parcel can carry, and what it can't.

It rides the infrastructure

E-commerce trafficking needs e-commerce logistics. Shade each country by its WCO region's average and the pattern is stark: the Americas at 68% against MENA at 1.5% and West & Central Africa at 0.6% — regions dominated by bulk tobacco and thinner postal networks.

So far this is a high-logistics-region phenomenon. It tracks parcel and postal maturity, not crime intensity.

So what

The 44.8% average is a targeting trap. Applied uniformly it over-invests where trade is already physical and under-defends where it is already online. With penetration ranging 0–82%, risk management has to be commodity-specific.

For the top cluster that means shifting weight from seaports and land borders to the postal and express hubs where the parcels actually move — and treating that as a systemic capability, not a specialist one.

Source: WCO Illicit Trade Report 2025 (data year 2024).
